What is I Drive Arkansas?

I Drive Arkansas is a smart web-based platform developed by the Arkansas Department of Transportation (ARDOT). It provides real-time information on road conditions, construction updates, weather impacts, traffic congestion, and travel advisories. Whether you're a resident commuting daily or a traveler exploring the state, I Drive Arkansas helps you stay informed and make better travel decisions.

Features of I Drive Arkansas

  • Live traffic flow maps
  • Construction zones and planned closures
  • Weather-related travel alerts
  • Rest area and weigh station locations
  • Access to highway cameras
  • Custom route planning

How to Access and Use It

Using I Drive Arkansas is simple. Here’s how:

  1. Go to the official website:
  2. Allow location services to get real-time data for your area.
  3. Use the map interface to explore traffic or weather updates.
  4. Select map layers to see cameras, closures, or rest stops.
  5. Click on alerts for more details and recommendations.

When Should You Use I Drive Arkansas?

You should check the platform before:

  • Leaving for work or appointments
  • Planning a road trip
  • Driving in bad weather (snow, rain, ice)
  • Passing through construction-prone areas

Mobile Access and Convenience

The website is fully mobile responsive. You can even save it to your home screen like an app. It loads quickly and is optimized for smartphones, making it easy to check travel conditions on the go.

Pro Tips

  • Bookmark frequently used routes for quicker access
  • Enable push notifications if supported for live alerts
  • Use it with your car’s navigation system or Google Maps

Support and Assistance

If you have trouble using the site or experience technical issues, you can get support directly from the live chat helpline. Just click the “Helpline” button above to connect instantly with support.

Conclusion

I Drive Arkansas is your smart driving partner. By giving you live access to critical road and traffic information, it helps you stay ahead of delays, avoid hazards, and travel safely across the state. Whether you're heading to Little Rock or crossing rural highways, make sure to check I Drive Arkansas before you go.
